SACHIN TENDULKAR

Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar born 24 April 1973 is an Indian former international cricketer who captained the Indian national team. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest batsmen in the history of cricket. He is the all-time highest run-scorer in both ODI and Test cricket with more than 18,000 runs and 15,000 runs, respectively. He also holds the record for receiving the most man-of-the-match awards in international cricket.Sachin was a Member of Parliament, Rajya Sabha by nomination from 2012 to 2018.

M S DHONI

Mahendra Singh Dhoni born 7 July 1981 is an Indian professional cricketer. He was captain of the Indian national team in limited-overs formats from 2007 to 2017 and in Test cricket from 2008 to 2014. He plays as a right-handed wicket-keeper-batsman and is also the current captain of Chennai Super Kings in the Indian Premier League.

VIRAT KOHLI

Virat Kohli is a legendary Indian cricketer, known for his exceptional batting skills, unwavering determination, and remarkable consistency. With a fearless approach and incredible record-breaking performances, he has established himself as one of the greatest batsmen of all time, inspiring millions with his passion and leadership.

ROHIT SHARMA

Rohit Sharma is a phenomenal Indian cricketer celebrated for his elegant batting style and ability to score big runs. With his impeccable timing and exquisite strokeplay, he has consistently dominated opposition bowlers across all formats of the game. Rohit's record-breaking innings and remarkable ability to score double centuries in ODIs have earned him a special place in cricket history.

KAPIL DEV

Kapil Dev Ramlal Nikhanj born 6 January 1959 is an Indian former cricketer. He was a fast-medium bowler and a hard-hitting middle-order batsman, and was named by Wisden as the Indian Cricketer of the Century in 2002. Dev captained the Indian cricket team that won the 1983 Cricket World Cup, and in the process became the first Indian captain to win the Cricket World Cup, and is still the youngest captain (at the age of 24) to win the World Cup for any team.

MOHINDER AMARNATH

Mohinder Amarnath Bhardwaj (born 24 September 1950) is a former Indian cricketer, cricket analyst and actor. He is the son of Lala Amarnath, the first post-independence captain of India. Mohinder was a member of the Indian team that won the 1983 Cricket World Cup. Mohinder is commonly mentioned by players and cricket pundits as the best Indian batsman against express pace.In 2009, he received the C. K. Nayudu Lifetime Achievement Award, the highest honour Indian board can bestow on a former player.

GAUTAM GAMBHIR

Gautam Gambhir born 14 October 1981 is an Indian politician, philanthropist and former cricketer, who has played all formats of the game. He played an integral role in India's win in two ICC Knock-outs. He is a current member of the Lok Sabha since 2019. He received the Padma Shri from the Government of India in 2019, the fourth highest civilian award in India

RAVINDRA JADEJA

Ravindrasinh Anirudhsinh Jadeja (born 6 December 1988), commonly known as Ravindra Jadeja, is an Indian international cricketer who represents the Indian national cricket team in all formats. He is an all-rounder, who bats left-handed and bowls left-arm orthodox spin. He was the captain of the Chennai Super Kings in the Indian Premier League. He represents Saurashtra in first-class cricket. He is regarded as one of the best all-rounders of the last decade.

KL RAHUL

KL Rahul is a talented Indian cricketer known for his stylish batting technique and versatility. He has consistently showcased his ability to adapt to different formats of the game, displaying remarkable consistency and a wide range of stroke play. With his skillful performances, Rahul has established himself as a valuable asset to the Indian cricket team.
